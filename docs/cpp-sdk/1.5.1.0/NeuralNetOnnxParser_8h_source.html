23 
24 === Loading NN architecture from ONNX file ===
25 Below are instructions for how an ONNX file should look like in order to define
26 a model with architecture that's being supported by Helayers.
27 
28 * The supported operators are "Conv", "AveragePool", "Gemm", "Pad", "Flatten",
29 "Concat" and "BatchNormalization", as well as operators that are interpreted as
30 an activation layer: "Pow", "Mul", "Sub" and "Constant". The "Add" operator is
31 also supported and may be used either as a separate layer that adds the outputs
32 of two previous layers together, or as part of an activation layer.
33 * The NN can be non-sequential: operator's output can be the input of multiple
34 operators, and the outputs of two operators can be added or concatenated to one
35 another as long as the shapes are compatible. The operators must form a DAG,
36 where all paths start and end in the same nodes.
37 * The NN must start with either a Conv or a Gemm operator.
38 * If both Conv/AveragePool and Gemm operators are present in a path along the
39 NN, all Conv/AveragePool operators must come before any Gemm operator, and a
40 Flatten operator must be present after the last Conv/AveragePool operator and
41 before the first Gemm operator. Between the Flatten operator and the first Gemm
42 operator that follows, the path must remain sequential.
43 * Flatten operator cannot be present in any case other than having both
44 bothConv/AveragePool and Gemm operators in a path along the NN.
45 * Pow, Mul, Add (unless adding two layers), Sub and Constant operators will be
46 interpreted as a polynomial activation layer. These operators may be present at
47 any location other than being the first layers of the NN.
48 * BatchNormalization operator must be applied with respect to the channels
49 dimension and must therefore be present only in NNs with Conv operator(s), and
50 not after any Flatten operator.
51 
52 *** Conv ***
53 Must have three inputs: the input data, filters and biases.
54 Must have one output.
55 The supported attributes are: (any other attribute will be ignored)
56  "kernel_shape" - required, must contain two integers
57  "strides" - required, must contain two integers
58  "pads" - required, must contain 4 integers
59 The number of filters will be deduced from the associated filters specified as
60 input for the operator.
61 Filters are assumed to be of shape [filters][channels][rows][cols]
62 
63 *** AveragePool ***
64 Must have one input: the input data.
65 Must have one output.
66 The supported attributes are: (any other attribute will be ignored)
67  "kernel_shape" - required, must contain two integers
68  "strides" - required, must contain two integers
69  "pads" - required, must contain 4 integers. Alternatively, pads will be taken
70  from a previous "Pad" operator, if such operator appears before this layer.
71 NOTICE: when an average pooling layer includes padding, its evaluation by
72 Helayers will include the pads when averaging, regardless of any setting or
73 behaviour of an external library.
74 
75 
76 *** Gemm ***
77 Must have three inputs: the input data, weights and bias.
78 Must have one output.
79 No attributes are supported (any attribute will be ignored).
80 The output size will be deduced from the associated weights specified as input
81 for the operator.
82 Weights are assumed to be of shape [output_size][input_size]
83 
84 *** Pow ***
85 Must have two inputs: the input data and the exponent.
86 Must have one output.
87 No attributes are supported (any attribute will be ignored).
88 The input associated with the exponent must contain a valid tensor with value.
89 There must be a single value and it must be a positive integer.
90 
91 *** Mul ***
92 Must have two inputs.
93 Must have one output.
94 No attributes are supported (any attribute will be ignored).
95 
96 *** Add ***
97 Must have two inputs.
98 Must have one output.
99 No attributes are supported (any attribute will be ignored).
100 
101 *** Sub ***
102 Must have two inputs.
103 Must have one output.
104 No attributes are supported (any attribute will be ignored).
105 
106 *** Constant ***
107 Must have no inputs.
108 Must have one output.
109 The supported attributes are: (any other attribute will be ignored)
110  "value" - required, must contain a valid tensor with a single value.
111 
112 *** Pad ***
113 Must have one input: the input data.
114 Must have one output.
115 The supported attributes are: (any other attribute will be ignored)
116  "pads" - required, must contain 8 integers: 0,0,T,L,0,0,B,R where T,L,B,R are
117  the paddings from top, left, bottom and right, and can be zero or non-zero
118  values. If provided, will be applied over the next layer which must be
119  AveragePool.
120 
121 *** Flatten ***
122 Must have one input: the input data.
123 Must have one output.
124 The supported attributes are: (any other attribute will be ignored)
125  "axis" - required, must contain 1 integer with a value of 1
126 
127 *** Concat ***
128 Must have two inputs.
129 Must have one output.
130 The supported attributes are: (any other attribute will be ignored)
131  "axis" - required, the axis to concatenate along, one of 1,2,3. Axis 1, 2 and
132  3 will be interpreted as the channels, rows and columns dimensions,
133  respectively.
134 
135 *** BatchNormalization ***
136 Must have five inputs: the input data, the scales, the biases, the means and the
137 variances.
138 Must have one output.
139 The supported attributes are: (any other attribute will be ignored)
140  "epsilon" - required, the value to use for the normalization, to avoid
141  deviding by zero.
142 The batch normalization is assumed to be over the channels dimension.
143 
144 */
